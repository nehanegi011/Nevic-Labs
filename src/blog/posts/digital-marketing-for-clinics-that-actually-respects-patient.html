Digital marketing for clinics that actually respects patients

Most marketing playbooks written for clinics were written for restaurants with a Google Business page.

They don't fit.

Restaurants sell moments. Clinics sell trust — earned slowly, lost quickly.

The playbook has to change.

Start with Google. A complete, up-to-date Business Profile with real photos, hours and doctors does more than any ad.

Then reviews. A one-tap NPS after every visit, with a friction-free path to Google for the happy ones, wins over months.

Then referrals. A tiny 'thank you' after a referred patient's first visit compounds faster than any campaign.

Then SEO. A monthly, honest post about a real question your patients ask — 'why does my knee hurt when it rains?' — beats a lifetime of paid ads.

Then WhatsApp broadcasts, if you must. Rarely. Never marketing; always news the patient can use.

Then Instagram, if the doctor enjoys it. Not because the algorithm wants it.

Then paid ads, last. Only after every organic channel is producing.

This order matters. Reversing it wastes money.

The bar for a healthcare ad should be: would I be proud if my mother saw this ad?

If not, don't run it.

The bar for a healthcare post should be: does this help a real patient with a real question?

If not, don't post it.

The bar for a review campaign should be: are you asking every patient, or only the ones you think will say yes?

The second is manipulation. Don't do it.

The bar for the whole marketing effort should be: does the patient trust us more after this?

Every campaign either earns that or doesn't. There's no third option.
