Rethinking appointment booking for Indian OPDs

Calendar-style booking assumes each slot is a promise. In most Indian OPDs, it isn't.

A slot is a hint. Patients arrive early, late, in groups, with unlisted family members, with cash instead of a card.

A rigid grid collapses under that reality within a week.

The right model for most Indian OPDs is a token-plus-slot hybrid.

The slot is the anchor: 'come between 11:15 and 11:30'.

The token is the order: 'you are T-13, T-12 is currently in the room'.

Together they give the patient a real ETA and give the doctor a real queue.

Neither the patient nor the doctor has to pretend the day will go exactly as planned.

Booking flows should mirror that.

The patient books a slot; the system assigns a token when they arrive.

Rescheduling should not require a phone call. Two taps in WhatsApp is the bar.

Cancellations should free the slot back to the pool immediately, so the next request can grab it.

Overbooking, done carefully, is not a sin. Every healthcare provider runs a small no-show buffer; the software should account for it explicitly.

The doctor should never see the buffer. The doctor should just see today's queue, in order, updating live.

The receptionist should be able to move any slot without breaking the token order.

The patient should always know their token number, even before they arrive.

The 'thank you for booking' message should feel like a real receipt: doctor, slot, location, what to bring.

The reminder should carry the reschedule and cancel buttons, so no one has to call.

The confirmation should update the queue in real time, so the front desk knows who's committed.

Get that loop tight and no-shows drop by half, on average, in the first month.
